Ph.D. Student at ETH Zurich

Research Interests:

My main interests lie on the intersection of NLP and AI with an emphasis on question answering systems. I am especially interested in challenges that occur after deployment as well as human-machine interactions. Particularly, I focus on: (1) continuous learning, complex NLP systems shouldn't be static but continue to learn over time; (2) human-machine interactions, extending the (natural language) interface between humans and machines in order to loop feedback back into the system; (3) low-resource settings, enabling deep-learning technologies in the absence of large-scale datasets.

In my free time, I am passionate about good food, running, and traveling.

About Me

I am a doctoral student at the Chair of Management Information System, ETH Zurich. I started in October 2017 working with Prof. Stefan Feuerriegel. In 2017 I visited the National Institute of Informatics in Tokyo as a visiting student.

Before starting my Ph.D. I received an MSc degree in Computer Science from ETH Zurich, where I wrote my master's thesis on unsupervised video generation. I received my BSc degree with distinction in Computer Science from the Technical University of Vienna.
